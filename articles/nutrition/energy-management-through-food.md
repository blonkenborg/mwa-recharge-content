Most knowledge workers experience a predictable energy curve: functional in the morning, declining after lunch, partially recovered in mid-afternoon, flagging by late afternoon. This pattern feels normal because it's common. It's not inevitable.

## The blood glucose connection

Energy fluctuation is largely governed by blood glucose. A meal high in rapidly digested carbohydrates causes a sharp glucose spike followed by a drop — the post-lunch crash.

A meal with more protein, fibre, and slower-digesting carbohydrates produces a flatter glucose curve and more stable energy.

## Practical shifts that make the biggest difference

**Anchor protein to every meal.** Protein is the most satiating macronutrient and has minimal effect on blood glucose.

**Reduce liquid sugar.** Sweetened coffee drinks, juice, and soft drinks cause the steepest glucose spikes. Switching to unsweetened versions is the single highest-leverage change for most people.

**Sequence your meals strategically.** Eating vegetables and protein first, carbohydrates last, measurably reduces post-meal glucose spikes — even with identical food compositions.

## Exercise as an energy regulator

Physical training directly improves insulin sensitivity — the efficiency with which cells extract glucose from the blood. Regular strength training reduces the amplitude of post-meal glucose swings over time, making the energy crash progressively less severe.

Your Recharge sessions aren't just building muscle. They're also recalibrating the metabolic system that governs your daily energy.
