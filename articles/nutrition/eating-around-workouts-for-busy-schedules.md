Nutrition timing is often presented as complex and demanding. For most people doing 20-40 minute strength sessions three to four times per week, it doesn't need to be.

## Pre-workout: the basics

Your body needs available energy to train effectively. If you're training more than 3 hours after your last meal, performance drops — not catastrophically, but meaningfully.

**What actually works for office workers:**
- Training late morning: a normal breakfast 2-3 hours before is sufficient
- Training at lunch: a light snack 60-90 minutes before
- Training mid-afternoon: lunch 2-3 hours prior, nothing else needed
- Avoid training on a completely empty stomach — the performance cost is real

## Post-workout: the window is real but forgiving

There is a 30-60 minute window post-training when the muscles are particularly receptive to amino acids. A protein-containing meal or snack in this window maximises recovery.

This doesn't have to be complicated: Greek yoghurt and fruit, or your normal lunch if it contains adequate protein (25-40g).

## The one non-negotiable

Total daily protein is far more important than timing. If you hit 1.6-2.2g of protein per kg of body weight per day, the timing optimisations are marginal.

For a 75kg person: aim for 120-165g of protein per day. Most whole food sources — eggs, chicken, fish, legumes, dairy — get you there without tracking obsessively.
