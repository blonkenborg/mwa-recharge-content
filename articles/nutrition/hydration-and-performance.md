Hydration is the most unsexy performance variable. It's also one of the most consistently underestimated.

## What dehydration actually does

A loss of just 1-2% of body weight in fluid produces a 5-10% reduction in strength output, impaired thermoregulation, reduced reaction time and concentration, and increased perception of effort (the same exercise feels harder).

Most people operate at mild chronic dehydration without realising it — particularly in climate-controlled office environments where the thirst signal is blunted.

## The problem with "drink when thirsty"

Thirst lags behind actual hydration status. By the time you feel thirsty, you are already slightly dehydrated.

## Practical targets

**General baseline:** 35ml per kg of body weight per day. For a 75kg person, that's about 2.6 litres.

**On training days:** add 500-750ml around the workout window — 200-300ml before, the rest during and after.

**Electrolytes matter too** — plain water is sufficient for sessions under 60 minutes. For longer sessions, a small amount of sodium aids absorption.

## The simplest system

- 500ml glass of water first thing in the morning
- A bottle on your desk (visible containers dramatically increase intake)
- 250-300ml before your Recharge session
- Continue drinking for 30 minutes post-session

Hydration pays compound interest on everything else you do.
