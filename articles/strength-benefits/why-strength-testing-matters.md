Most people see a strength test as a measurement. The EGYM system uses it as something fundamentally more powerful: a calibration input.

## What the test actually measures

A strength test on EGYM equipment assesses **maximum voluntary force** in a controlled movement pattern. This data point establishes the precise resistance level that will produce the optimal training stimulus — not too easy (adaptation stops), not too heavy (injury risk increases, form breaks down).

## Autoregulation: training that adapts to you

The concept of **autoregulation** — where training intensity adjusts based on real performance data — is the gold standard in advanced strength programming. Elite coaches use subjective autoregulation (adjusting by feel); the EGYM system does it objectively, based on actual test results.

## Why testing frequency matters

Strength adapts faster than most people expect, especially in the early months of training. A test result from 8 weeks ago may no longer represent current capacity — meaning training at that level is suboptimal. Regular testing keeps the calibration current.

## The psychological dimension

Seeing strength improve in measurable, objective numbers has a documented positive effect on self-efficacy — the belief in one's ability to execute a behavior. Higher self-efficacy is one of the strongest predictors of long-term exercise adherence (Bandura, 1997).

## How this connects to your training

The strength test challenge isn't a formality. It's the input that makes every other session more effective. Completing it regularly is one of the highest-leverage things possible within the Recharge system.
