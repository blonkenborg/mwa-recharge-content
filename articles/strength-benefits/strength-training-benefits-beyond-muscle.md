When most people think about strength training, they picture aesthetics. But the most important effects are happening where you can't see them.

## Bone density

Resistance training is one of the most powerful stimuli for bone formation. As the skeleton is loaded, osteoblasts — the cells that build bone — become more active. This matters at every age, and the WHO identifies osteoporosis as a major global health burden that strength training directly counters.

## Metabolic health

Muscle is metabolically expensive tissue. Every kilogram of muscle added increases resting metabolic rate — meaning more energy burned even at rest. This compounds significantly over time.

## Mental health

Multiple large meta-analyses have confirmed that resistance training significantly reduces symptoms of depression and anxiety. Mechanisms include endorphin release, improved self-efficacy, and regulation of the HPA (stress) axis.

## Longevity

Grip strength — a proxy for overall muscle strength — is one of the strongest predictors of all-cause mortality in longitudinal studies. Stronger people live longer, independent of cardiovascular fitness. A 2018 *Lancet* study across 17 countries confirmed this relationship.

## How this connects to your training

Every set completed in the weekly strength challenge contributes to all of the above. It's not just a number to hit — it's a real investment in how long and how well you live.
