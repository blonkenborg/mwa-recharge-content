Willpower is finite. Environment is not. The most durable fitness habits are built not by trying harder, but by making the desired behavior easier than the alternative.

## The visibility principle

Behavioral economist Brian Wansink demonstrated that people consume more of whatever is most visible and accessible — a principle that applies to behavior, not just food. **What is easy to see and start becomes what gets done.**

Applied to fitness: equipment in sight, app on the home screen, gym on the walking route — all of these reduce the decision cost to near zero.

## Friction reduction

Every step between the intention to exercise and the start of exercise is an opportunity to abandon the habit. **Removing one step** can dramatically change follow-through rates.

Strategies:
- Keep workout clothes at the office
- Pre-set the EGYM app to the workout screen before leaving the desk
- Identify which specific machines will be used before arriving

## Context cues

Environments trigger behaviors. Sitting at the same desk at the same time every day eventually makes the desk a cue for "starting the workday." The same effect can be created for exercise: using the same entry point, the same sequence of machines, the same time of day trains the nervous system to expect and initiate the workout automatically.

## How this connects to your training

The Recharge workout is already environment-design-friendly — the equipment is in the building, the sessions are short, and the app reduces friction to a single tap. The remaining variable is making the cue consistent enough to become automatic.
