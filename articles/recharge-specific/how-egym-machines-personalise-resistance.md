A traditional gym machine has a weight stack and a pin. Everyone uses the same stack; individuals choose the pin position. The EGYM system works differently.

## Isokinetic resistance

Many EGYM machines use isokinetic resistance — a system that adapts resistance in real time to match the force you're applying, maintaining a consistent speed of movement regardless of how hard you push.

With a fixed weight, there are parts of any movement where the load feels easier and harder. Isokinetic resistance stays challenging throughout the entire range of motion — meaning more time under effective tension, which is a key driver of hypertrophy.

## Personalisation to your profile

The resistance levels on your machines are derived from your most recent strength test. The system knows your 1RM on each movement and sets your training load as a percentage of that — targeting the specific training zone you're working in.

As you get stronger, the absolute resistance increases automatically at the next test cycle. You never have to think about whether you're working at the right intensity.

## What this means in practice

Two colleagues using the same machine at the same time will be working at different resistance levels — calibrated to their individual strength profiles. The machine isn't giving one person an advantage; it's giving both of them the optimal training stimulus for where they are.

This is the key difference between the EGYM system and conventional machines. The personalisation is handled automatically, and it updates every time you test.
