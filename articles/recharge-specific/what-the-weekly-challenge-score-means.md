The Recharge weekly challenge tracks three metrics. They're not interchangeable — each measures a different dimension of training quality.

## Workout days

**What it measures:** Frequency. How many days in the week did you show up?

**Why it matters:** Consistency is the strongest predictor of long-term fitness outcomes. Target: 3 days per week is the well-validated minimum for meaningful adaptation.

## Exercise sets

**What it measures:** Volume. Total number of sets completed across sessions.

**Why it matters:** Volume is the primary driver of hypertrophy. More total sets (within recovery capacity) means more training stimulus. This metric rewards completing full sessions, not just showing up.

Target: 10-20 sets per muscle group per week is the effective range for most people.

## Distinct exercises

**What it measures:** Variety. How many different movements did you train?

**Why it matters:** Consistently training only a few exercises creates imbalances. The full EGYM circuit covers the major movement patterns — push, pull, hinge, squat — ensuring balanced development.

## How to use all three

If you're consistently behind on one metric, it indicates a specific gap. A high sets score with low distinct exercises means you're doing a lot of the same thing. High workout days with low sets means you're showing up but cutting sessions short. The metrics disaggregate what "training consistently" actually means.
