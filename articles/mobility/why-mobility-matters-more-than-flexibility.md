Flexibility and mobility are often used interchangeably. They're not the same thing, and the distinction matters for how you train and move.

**Flexibility** is the capacity of a muscle to lengthen passively — measured by how far you can stretch when an external force assists the movement.

**Mobility** is the ability to actively move a joint through its full range of motion under your own muscular control. It requires not just length in the tissues, but strength, coordination, and neuromuscular control throughout that range.

## Why the distinction matters

A hypermobile person can put their palms flat on the floor. But if they lack the active strength to control hip hinge movements under load, they are actually at elevated injury risk — their joints can access ranges their muscles can't protect.

This is why static stretching alone is a poor injury prevention strategy. Research consistently shows it has minimal impact on injury rates when performed in isolation.

## What actually improves functional range

**Strength through full range of motion.** A deep squat trains hip flexion and ankle dorsiflexion under load — producing both flexibility and the active control to use it safely.

**Compound strength training.** The EGYM machines, performed through full available range with appropriate load, double as mobility training for the major joint complexes.

## Practical takeaway

Prioritise strength through full range of motion, and mobility will follow. The person who consistently squats deeply under load is more mobile — in the functional sense — than someone who stretches daily but never trains.
