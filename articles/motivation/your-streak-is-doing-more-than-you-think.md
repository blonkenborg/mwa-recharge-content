That dot is alive because you kept showing up.

Here's what's actually happening underneath it: every consistent week of training is compounding. Muscle fibers are slightly denser. Neural pathways for the movement patterns are more efficient. The habit loop — cue, routine, reward — is more automatic than it was last month.

**Neuroscience calls this myelination.** Each time a behavior repeats, the nerve pathways involved get wrapped in a faster-conducting sheath. The trained brain literally becomes more wired for the habit. After enough repetitions, the action stops requiring a decision — it just happens.

Your streak isn't tracking discipline. It's tracking the physical remodeling of your nervous system.

**One more week and that wiring gets a little stronger.**
