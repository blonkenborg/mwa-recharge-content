Most people assume that exercise only counts if you're in the gym for at least 45 minutes. Science says otherwise.

Research consistently shows that **accumulating short bouts of physical activity** — even 5 to 10 minutes at a time — delivers measurable cardiovascular and metabolic benefits. A 2018 study in the *Journal of the American Heart Association* found that three 10-minute walks per day were as effective as one 30-minute walk for reducing blood pressure.

## What happens in your body

Even a brief bout of strength training triggers muscle protein synthesis, improves insulin sensitivity, and releases endorphins. The dose-response curve for exercise is steep at the low end — meaning the first 10 minutes of effort produce disproportionately large benefits compared to minutes 40 through 50.

## Consistency beats duration

The real superpower of micro workouts is sustainability. A habit you can keep is infinitely more valuable than a perfect routine you abandon. By making each session short enough to feel manageable, the biggest barrier to exercise — getting started — disappears.
