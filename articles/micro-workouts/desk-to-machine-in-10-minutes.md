A 10-minute strength session is not a consolation prize. Done correctly, it's a complete training stimulus. Here's the workflow.

## The 10-minute structure

| Time | Action |
|---|---|
| 0:00–1:00 | Walk to machine, log in with band |
| 1:00–2:00 | First exercise — warm-up set (60% effort) |
| 2:00–4:30 | First exercise — working set |
| 4:30–5:00 | Move to next machine |
| 5:00–7:30 | Second exercise — working set |
| 7:30–8:00 | Move to third machine |
| 8:00–10:00 | Third exercise — working set, log out |

Three working sets in 10 minutes. That's a real training session.

## Why minimal rest isn't a problem

For hypertrophy (muscle growth), rest periods of 60–90 seconds are effective. The EGYM machines facilitate this naturally — moving between machines is active recovery. Short rest periods may even produce additional metabolic stress, which is a secondary driver of muscle growth.

## The mental model shift

The barrier is never 10 minutes. The barrier is the decision to start. Once the decision is made and the band is on, the session is effectively complete. The body handles the rest automatically.
