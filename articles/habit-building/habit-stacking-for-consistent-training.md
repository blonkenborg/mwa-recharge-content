In *Atomic Habits*, James Clear popularized a deceptively simple idea: don't try to build new habits from scratch. Attach them to habits you already have.

This is called **habit stacking**, and it works because existing habits are already wired into neural circuitry. Attaching a new behavior to an existing cue bypasses the friction of having to remember or motivate yourself.

## The formula

> After/Before [CURRENT HABIT], I will [NEW HABIT].

Examples:
- "After I pour my morning coffee, I will do 10 squats."
- "Before I sit down at my desk, I will do one set on the machine."
- "After I take the elevator to my floor, I will take the stairs back down."

## Why it works neurologically

Habits are stored in the basal ganglia — a region associated with procedural memory. Once a behavior becomes automatic, it requires almost no conscious effort. Habit stacking accelerates this by borrowing the automaticity of an existing routine.

## How this connects to your training

The Recharge workout fits naturally into a gap that already exists in the workday. Identify one moment that reliably happens — a meeting break, lunch, a specific time — and anchor the workout to it. Within a few weeks, the anchor does the remembering.
