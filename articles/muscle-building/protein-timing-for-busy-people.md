Nutrition advice for strength athletes is often presented as complex and demanding. The actual evidence is much more practical.

## The one number that matters

Total daily protein intake is the most important nutritional variable for muscle growth. The current evidence-based recommendation is **1.6–2.2g of protein per kg of body weight per day** (Morton et al., 2018, *British Journal of Sports Medicine*). For an 80kg person, that's 128–176g per day.

Timing, protein quality, and specific foods matter far less than hitting this total.

## How to reach the target without effort

A few anchor foods make this achievable without tracking:
- 200g chicken breast: ~48g protein
- 2 eggs: ~12g protein
- 200g Greek yogurt: ~20g protein
- 100g cottage cheese: ~11g protein
- One protein shake: 20–30g protein

Two high-protein meals and a snack can cover the daily target.

## Post-workout protein: how important is it?

The "anabolic window" (the idea that protein must be consumed within 30 minutes of training) has been largely debunked. The more recent consensus (Aragon & Schoenfeld, 2013) is that total daily intake matters far more than timing — as long as protein is distributed across meals rather than consumed entirely in one sitting.

## How this connects to your training

Micro workouts create a training stimulus. Protein provides the raw material for the adaptation. Together, they produce results. Neither is sufficient alone.
